Environmental Pollution

What is Pollution?

Pollution is the introduction of contaminants into the environment that cause harm or discomfort to living organisms. These contaminants, known as pollutants, can be chemical substances, noise, heat, or light, and they can have adverse effects on the environment and human health.

Types of Pollution

1. Air Pollution: Contamination of the atmosphere by harmful gases and particles, resulting in respiratory illnesses and global warming.
2. Water Pollution: Introduction of waste into water bodies like lakes and rivers, disrupting ecosystems and causing diseases.
3. Soil Pollution: Degradation of land by chemicals, which reduces fertility and can contaminate food crops.
Main Causes of Pollution
1. Burning Fossil Fuels: Combustion for energy releases carbon monoxide and nitrogen oxides into the air.
2. Industrial Activities: Factories emit heavy metals and chemicals into the air, water, and soil.
3. Agricultural Practices: Pesticides and fertilizers lead to soil degradation and water runoff contamination.

Health Impacts

• Respiratory Issues: Inhaled pollutants worsen illnesses such as asthma and bronchitis.
• Cardiovascular Issues: Fine particles can enter the bloodstream, increasing heart risks.
• Neurological Damage: Heavy metals like lead can cause developmental problems in children.
